DevStride keeps help close at hand. The **Help** section at the bottom of the left sidebar gives you quick access to documentation and a way to reach the DevStride team without leaving your work.

## The Help Section

Look at the bottom of the left navigation sidebar. The **Help** section contains three links:

- **Connect AI (MCP)** — opens the setup for connecting AI assistants to your DevStride workspace.
- **Learn** — opens the DevStride documentation site.
- **Support** — opens a support request form.

The **Help** section appears for everyone on your team, so any user can reach documentation and support regardless of their role.

## Learn

Click **Learn** to open the DevStride documentation site at [docs.devstride.com](https://docs.devstride.com). This is the same library of guides and articles you are reading now. The link opens in a new browser tab, so you can keep your workspace open while you read.

You can also reach **Learn** from the command palette. Press `Cmd + K` (or `Ctrl + K` on Windows) to open the keyboard search, then look for the **Learn** command — described as "Documentation and guides to help you with DevStride." Selecting it opens the documentation site in a new tab.

::alert{type="tip" title="Fast access to docs"}
The command palette (`Cmd/Ctrl + K`) is the quickest way to jump to documentation from anywhere in DevStride. The **Learn** command lives there alongside your other quick actions.
::

## Support

Click **Support** to open the DevStride request form in a new browser tab. Use this form whenever you need to reach the DevStride team — to report a problem, ask a question, or request help.

The Support form is a public request form, so you do not need to sign in again to submit it. Fill in the details of your request and submit it. Your request is sent directly to the DevStride team.

::alert{type="info" title="Support is sidebar-only"}
The **Support** link is available from the **Help** section of the sidebar. Unlike **Learn**, it does not appear in the command palette, so head to the sidebar when you want to submit a request.
::

## Connect AI (MCP)

The **Help** section also includes **Connect AI (MCP)**, the entry point for connecting AI assistants to your DevStride workspace. Setup and details are covered separately in [Connect AI & the MCP Server](/settings/integrations/connect-ai-and-mcp).
