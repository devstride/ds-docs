![Weekly Logs Filters](/images/weekly-logs/filters.png)

Your timesheet can grow long quickly. Filters let you cut through the noise and focus on what matters right now.

- Click **Filters** in the toolbar to open the filter panel. 
- A panel opens with the available filter types. 
- Select a filter type to open its selector, make your selections, and click **Apply**. 
- The grid immediately narrows to show only matching rows.

## Items

Filter by item number or name.

1. Click **Items** in the filter panel.
2. Type a keyword or item number and press **Enter** to add it as a chip.
3. Add as many items as needed, then click **Apply**.

Rows that do not match any of the selected items are hidden. Search by keyword to match anything containing that word in the name, or use item numbers to filter to exact items.

## Workstream

Filter by folder or hierarchy path.

1. Click **Workstream** in the filter panel.
2. Browse or search the folder tree and select a workstream.
3. Click **Apply**.

Only tasks within the selected workstream path remain visible.

## Team

Filter by team assignment.

1. Click **Team** in the filter panel.
2. Select one or more teams from the list.
3. Click **Apply**.

Only tasks belonging to the selected teams are shown.

## Assignee

**Available in Team Logs mode only.**

1. Click **Assignee** in the filter panel.
2. Select one or more team members from the list.
3. Click **Apply**.

The grid filters down to only those users and the tasks they have logged time on. See [Team Logs](/plugins/weekly-logs/team-logs).

## Organizations

**Available in All Orgs mode only.**

Filter down to a specific organization when viewing across multiple. See [Org switch](/plugins/weekly-logs/org-switch).

## Combining Filters

You can combine any of these filter types. Each filter you add narrows the grid further — a row must match **all** active filters to remain visible.

Within a single filter type, selecting multiple values shows rows matching **any** of those values.

## Removing Filters

Active filters appear as chips above the grid once applied.

- Click **X** on a chip to remove that filter category.
- Click **Clear All** to reset all filters at once.
