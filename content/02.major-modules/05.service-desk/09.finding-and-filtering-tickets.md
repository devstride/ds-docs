title: "Finding & Filtering Tickets"
description: "Because tickets are work items, you can find and report on support work anywhere in DevStride — filter by requester, company, channel, customer importance, or SLA state, add triage columns, and save the views you return to."
---

One of the quiet benefits of tickets being ordinary work items is that you don't need a separate reporting tool for support. Every place you already filter, sort, save views, and report on items understands tickets too.

## The Support filter

Across the app's item tables and filter bars, a **Support** filter lets you narrow to tickets (or exclude them). Use it to answer questions like *"show me only support work"* on a shared board, or *"hide tickets"* when you want to look at delivery work on its own.

The Service Desk **Inbox** applies this automatically — it's permanently scoped to tickets — but the Support filter is available elsewhere so you can slice support work into any view.

## Filtering by support dimensions

When you're looking at tickets, the filter bar adds support-specific dimensions on top of the usual ones (owner, status, priority, dates, labels, custom fields):

- **Requester** — narrow to a specific customer.
- **Company** — see everything from one customer organization.
- **Channel** — filter by how the request arrived.
- **Customer Importance** — narrow to tickets marked **Critical**, **Important**, or **Nice to have**.
- **SLA status** — slice by where a ticket stands against its SLA (for example, tickets that are running, at risk, or breached).

Combine these with the standard filters to build precise queries — *"open, Critical tickets from Acme, unassigned, raised this week,"* for example. Both of the new dimensions are also available in the advanced condition-builder, so they can go into any complex saved view.

## Ticket columns

Add support columns to your item tables to see the information that matters for triage — the requester and company alongside status, owner, and priority — so a queue reads at a glance.

Alongside the requester and company, four service-desk columns bring a ticket's urgency into the table. Each is **sortable**, so you can order a queue by what matters most right now:

- **Customer Importance** — the triage level set on the ticket: **Critical**, **Important**, or **Nice to have**.
- **Customer Rank** — the manual order a customer's tickets have been ranked in. The cell shows a **Ranked** marker; sort by this column to put the queue in ranked order.
- **SLA Due** — when the ticket's SLA deadline falls, so the closest-to-breaching work rises to the top.
- **First Response** — when the ticket first received a public reply (blank until one is sent).

These columns are hidden by default — add them from the column picker on any items table. Sorting by **SLA Due** is the fastest way to build a "what's about to breach" queue; sorting by **Customer Importance** or **Customer Rank** puts your highest-value work first.

## Save and share views

Capture any filtered, sorted, columned setup as a **saved view** and reuse it, or share it with teammates — exactly as you would for any items view. A few that teams find useful:

- **Unassigned tickets** — what still needs an owner.
- **My open tickets** — your personal queue.
- **A customer's requests** — filtered to one company, for an account review.

::alert{type="tip" title="Report on support like any work"}
Because tickets flow through boards and cycles like other items, your existing reports — throughput, cycle time, cumulative flow, and the rest — work on support too. Filter a report to tickets to measure how your support queue is trending. See [Measure Performance: Reports](/measure-performance-reports).
::
