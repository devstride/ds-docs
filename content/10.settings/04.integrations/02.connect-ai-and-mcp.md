DevStride ships a built-in **Model Context Protocol (MCP) server** so you can connect AI assistants like Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, and Copilot directly to your workspace. Once connected, your assistant can answer questions about your work and make changes on your behalf using natural language.

::alert{type="info" title="This now has its own section"}
The MCP Server is documented in full under **[DevStride MCP Server](/devstride-mcp-server/what-is-the-devstride-mcp-server)**. Start there:

- **[What Is the DevStride MCP Server?](/devstride-mcp-server/what-is-the-devstride-mcp-server)** — what it is and what it unlocks
- **[Setting Up Your Connection](/devstride-mcp-server/setting-up-your-connection)** — connect Claude, ChatGPT, and other clients
- **[Doing Real Work with the DevStride MCP Server](/devstride-mcp-server/doing-real-work-with-the-devstride-mcp-server)** — prompts and a worked example
- **[Best Practices & Tips](/devstride-mcp-server/best-practices-and-tips)**
::

You reach the connection screen from the **Help** section of the left sidebar — **Connect AI (MCP)** — where you'll find your MCP Server URL and ready-to-paste setup for each client.
