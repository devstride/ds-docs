---
title: Link Mode
description: Automatically shift related item dates down a dependency chain when you change an item's due date.
---

Link Mode keeps your dates in sync across linked work. When it is on, changing an item's due date automatically moves the start and due dates of everything that item blocks, cascading down the dependency chain so your plan stays consistent without manual rework.

Link Mode is an organization-wide setting, so it applies to every board, folder, and item across your organization at once. There is no per-board or per-item toggle.

## What Link Mode does

When Link Mode is **on** and you change an item's **due date**, DevStride calculates how many days the due date moved and applies that same shift to **both the start date and the due date** of every item that the changed item blocks. The shift then cascades recursively: each of those downstream items passes the same offset on to whatever it blocks, and so on through the entire chain.

A few specifics worth knowing:

- **Only the due date triggers the cascade.** Changing an item's due date is what starts the propagation. Editing only the start date does not start a cascade, even though items that *are* shifted have both their start and due dates moved.
- **Only "blocks" dependencies propagate.** The cascade follows blocking dependencies (the relationship where one item blocks another). Items connected only by a "related to" link are not moved.
- **The shift is a calendar-day offset.** Dates move by the exact number of calendar days the due date changed. The shift does not skip weekends or account for working days.
- **It works for items, folders, workstreams, and milestones.** Anything downstream in the blocking chain is shifted. Items, folders, and workstreams have both their start and due dates moved; milestones, which have only a due date, have their due date shifted.
- **Propagation runs in the background.** The downstream date changes are processed by a background job, so they may appear a moment after you make your edit rather than instantly.

The change you make to one item flows automatically to everything it blocks, so a slipped due date moves the dependent work along with it instead of leaving the schedule out of date.

## What happens when Link Mode is off

When Link Mode is **off**, DevStride does not automatically move related dates. Instead, if a date change would conflict with related items, DevStride shows a **Relationships Date Warning** dialog asking you to **Confirm** or **Cancel** the change. You stay in control of each edit, but you also have to adjust downstream dates yourself.

Turning Link Mode **on** suppresses that warning dialog and lets the dates auto-adjust instead.

## Turning Link Mode on or off

::alert{type="info" title="Admin only"}
Only organization Admins (and the Owner) can open the settings page that hosts this control. If you are not an Admin, you will be redirected away from it.
::

::alert{type="note" title="On by default"}
Link Mode is enabled by default for organizations. If you do not want dates to shift automatically, open the setting and uncheck it. Do not assume it is off until you have verified it.
::

To find and change the setting:

1. Open **Settings**.
2. In the sidebar, select **Org Configuration**.
3. Scroll to the **Workflow Automation** section.
4. Check or uncheck **Enable Link Mode**.

The **Enable Link Mode** checkbox includes a tooltip that reads: *"Allow item actions to propagate down the dependency chain."*
