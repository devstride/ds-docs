DevStride plugins are purpose-built extensions that bring additional workflows directly into the platform. They are designed for teams that need more than core project management — without the overhead of managing separate tools.

## What are Plugins

Plugins are optional extensions. They run natively inside DevStride — they have direct access to your workstreams, tasks, teams, and users, and load within the same interface you already use.

There is no separate account, no separate login, and no context switching. Everything a plugin needs — your organization, your assigned work, your team structure — is already available.

## Why Plugins Exist

The core DevStride platform is built around planning, tracking, and delivering work. Not every team needs every capability, and not every workflow belongs in the core product.

Plugins address the workflows that sit alongside the work itself — such as time tracking, resource reporting, or team productivity tooling. Teams can enable what is relevant to them and leave out what is not, keeping the platform focused and uncluttered.

## How to Enable a Plugin

Plugins are enabled on a per-organization basis by the DevStride team. To activate a plugin for your organization, reach out to DevStride support with your request.

Once enabled, the plugin appears under **Plugins** in the left navigation for all members of your organization.

::alert{title="What to Include in Your Request" type="info"}
Let us know which plugin you need and the name of your organization. The DevStride team will confirm once it has been activated.
::
