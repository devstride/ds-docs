## 🎧 Introducing Service Desk

Support and delivery have always lived in two different tools. **Service Desk** brings them together: a customer emails your support address, it becomes a **ticket** in DevStride, and your team triages and replies to it — over email — without ever leaving the app. Because a ticket is just a work item with a customer attached, it sits right next to the work it depends on and flows through the same boards, filters, and reports you already use.

This is **Version 1**, focused on **two-way email support**.

### ✉️ What you can do

- **Receive email as tickets.** Forward your support inbox to a DevStride channel address and every message becomes a ticket, automatically filed where you want it.
- **Reply over email, in context.** Send a **Reply to customer** from the ticket; it's emailed to them and their response threads right back onto the same conversation.
- **Keep internal notes private.** Every ticket carries two streams — customer replies and internal notes — and the line between them is enforced everywhere, so private discussion never leaks.
- **Manage requesters & companies.** Customers are tracked as requesters (who never use a paid seat), grouped into companies, with per-company defaults for routing and ownership. Merge duplicates or erase a requester on request.
- **Triage with confidence.** A dedicated **Inbox** queues your tickets, a **Suspended** queue holds back spam and auto-replies for review, and the **Support** filter lets you slice support work anywhere in the app.
- **Control access with roles.** Four Service Desk permissions — and a ready-made **Service Desk Agent** role — decide who can configure the desk, manage requesters, and reply to customers.

### 🚀 Getting started

1. **Turn it on.** An admin opens **Settings → Service Desk** and clicks **Enable Service Desk**.
2. **Create a channel address.** Under **Channel Addresses**, click **+ Channel**, pick the address, and set the **default location** and **work type** for new tickets.
3. **Forward your inbox.** Copy the new address and forward `support@yourcompany.com` to it.
4. **Work your first ticket.** Open **Service Desk → Inbox**, click the new ticket, and send a **Reply to customer**. That's the loop.
