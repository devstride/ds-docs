## 🌐 The Customer Support Portal Arrives

Service Desk started as email. Now your customers have a home of their own: a **branded, self-service Customer Support Portal** at your company's own web address. It's a fast, mobile-friendly page that lives separately from your team's DevStride workspace — your customers sign in there, and only there, and can never reach your boards, your data, or anyone else's company.

This first release delivers the **portal itself and how customers get in**: branded sign-in, passwordless login, and per-company access controls. The in-portal experience where customers browse and manage their requests is coming next (see **What's next** below).

### 🔑 Passwordless Sign-In

No passwords, ever. Getting into the portal takes seconds:

1. Your customer opens the portal and enters their **email address**.
2. They receive **both a magic link and a 6-digit code** by email.
3. They **click the link** on that device, or **type the code** — whichever is handier.
4. They're in, with a session that lasts about **a week** before they need to sign in again.

Security is built in quietly. The response after entering an email is **identical whether or not that address can access your portal**, so the portal can never be used to fish for who your customers are. Login links and codes **expire after 15 minutes**, and a code is retired after a handful of wrong guesses.

### 🎨 Make It Yours — Per-Company Branding

Each company you support gets its own portal look, set right on the **Company** record under **Service Desk → Companies**:

- **Logo** — your customer's own logo at the top of the page.
- **Header text** — a welcome line in your words.
- **Primary color** — the accent color that carries through the portal.

Set nothing and the portal falls back to a clean, neutral DevStride shell — so it always looks finished. Every portal carries a discreet "Powered by DevStride" in the footer.

### 🚪 Decide Who Can Sign In

Also on the Company record, choose an **access mode** that fits how open you want the portal to be:

- **Open** — anyone with a valid email can sign in.
- **Domain-restricted** — only people on that company's approved email domains.
- **Invite only** — only requesters your team has already added.

Access is re-checked on **every visit**, so it's always current. The moment you block a requester, erase them, move them to a different company, or turn Service Desk off, their access to the portal ends **immediately** — no waiting for a session to time out.

### 🌍 Default Language

The portal **auto-detects each visitor's language** from their browser, and you can set a **Default language** on the company record as a fallback. **English** is available today, with the groundwork in place to add more languages down the road.

## 🔒 Stronger Privacy — Hardened Requester Erasure

Erasing a requester ("right to be forgotten") is now **thorough and final**. When you erase someone, DevStride removes their personal information **everywhere it was kept** — their name, email, and phone; the original message that started their ticket; their personal details inside notifications and the activity log; and anything tied to their portal sign-in.

An erased requester **cannot be quietly brought back** — a later email from that person won't silently re-create or re-identify them. Tickets your team worked on are **kept, but stripped of who they belonged to**, so your history stays intact without the personal data.

::alert{type="caution" title="Erasure is permanent"}
Erasing a requester **cannot be undone or reversed**. Once it's done, the personal data is gone for good. Reach for **Merge** instead when you're consolidating duplicates you want to keep.
::

## 🛡️ Quieter, Safer Public Pages

We also tightened protection on the public-facing parts of Service Desk — the request form and portal sign-in — so bursts of automated or abusive traffic are throttled before they reach your team. There's nothing to configure; it just runs.
