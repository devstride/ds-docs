## 🎫 Service Desk: Sharper Triage & Ticket Management

This release builds out the day-to-day of running support — making it faster to see what's urgent, prioritize it, and keep your queue clean.

### ⭐ Customer Importance

Tickets now carry a **Customer Importance** — a quick triage signal separate from priority, with three levels: **Critical**, **Important**, and **Nice to have**. Set it on a ticket as you triage, or select several in the Inbox and set them **in bulk**. Importance isn't just a label — you can sort and filter on it, and match **SLA policies** against it, so it feeds straight into how tickets surface and how their deadlines are set.

### 📊 New Triage Columns

Four service-desk columns bring a ticket's urgency right into your item tables, and every one is **sortable**:

- **Customer Importance** — Critical / Important / Nice to have.
- **Customer Rank** — the manual order a customer's tickets are ranked in.
- **SLA Due** — when the SLA deadline falls, so the closest-to-breaching work rises to the top.
- **First Response** — when the ticket first got a public reply.

Sorting by **SLA Due** is the quickest way to build a "what's about to breach" queue; sorting by **Customer Importance** or **Customer Rank** puts your highest-value work first. Add any of them from the column picker.

### 🔎 New Filters

The filter bar (and the advanced condition-builder) gains two support dimensions on top of Requester, Company, and Channel:

- **Customer Importance** — narrow to just Critical work, for example.
- **SLA status** — slice by where tickets stand against their SLA.

Combine them into precise, saveable views like *"open, Critical tickets from Acme, unassigned, raised this week."*

### 🔗 Merge Duplicate Tickets

When the same issue arrives twice — an email plus a form, or two people reporting one outage — open the ticket menu and choose **Merge**, then pick which ticket **survives**. The duplicate's conversation and history fold onto the survivor, leaving one clean thread instead of scattered fragments.

### 🧩 Ticket Context Everywhere

Service-desk information now travels with tickets beyond the ticket view — a requester subtitle and an **SLA chip** on board cards, and ticket fields in **global search**, **CSV export**, and the **archive**. Exports also respect your ticket filters, so a filtered queue exports exactly what you're looking at.

### 🏢 Companies Gather Existing Requesters

When you create a company with an email domain, any requesters you've **already** been talking to on that domain are linked to it right away — so standing up a company retroactively pulls in the customers you already have, not just future ones.
