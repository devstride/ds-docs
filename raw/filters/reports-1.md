# Reports

> 

## Filters in the "Reports" Section of DevStride

The **Reports** section in DevStride is instrumental for tracking and analyzing various aspects of project performance. Different reports offer unique insights, and filters are key to customizing these reports. Below is a guide on how to use filters for each type of report in this section.

## Common Filters for All Reports

Most reports share a set of common filters which include:

- **Parent**: Show results related to a parent item.
- **Item Type**: Filter by type, like Story, Bug, Epic, etc.
- **Team**: Display data for a specific team.
- **Assignee**: Filter results based on assignee.
- **Priority**: Show items based on priority levels.
- **Tag**: Filter by tags assigned to items.
- **Field**: Use custom fields to filter results.
- **Cycle**: Display data for items in a specific cycle.
- **Board**: Filter by the board status of items.
- **Start Date**: Filter items based on the start date.
- **Due Date**: Display items by due date.
- **Time Spent**: Show data based on the time logged against items.

## Additional Filters for Specific Reports

### Trending Progress and Churn Report

For the Trending Progress and Churn reports, an additional filter available is:

- **Status**: This filter allows you to narrow down the data based on the status of items. You can select statuses such as New, In Progress, Review, Pending Release, etc., to provide a focused view of item churn or progress within the selected statuses.

## Applying Filters to Reports

To apply filters to any report:

1. Navigate to the **Analytics** section in DevStride.
2. Choose a report type from the analytics menu on the left.
3. Click the `Filters` button on the top-right of the report.
4. Select the desired filters and set the criteria.
5. The report will update to reflect the data based on the selected filters.

## Clearing Filters

To clear filters from a report:

1. For individual filter removal, click the `X` next to the filter tag at the top of the report.
2. To clear all filters, click the `Filters` button and choose `Clear All` from the dropdown menu.

The report will then revert to showing data without any filter applied.

## Summary

Utilizing filters in the Reports section allows teams to gain targeted insights and make data-driven decisions. By applying and adjusting filters, teams can focus on specific metrics that align with their strategic objectives.
